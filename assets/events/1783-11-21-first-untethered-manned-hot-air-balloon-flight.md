In 1783, Jean-François Pilâtre de Rozier and the Marquis d'Arlandes became the first humans to achieve sustained flight. Their balloon was powered by a smoky fire of straw and wool. 

They flew for 25 minutes over Paris, reaching a height of 3,000 feet. It was the first time humans had truly broken the bonds of gravity. Benjamin Franklin, who was in the audience, was asked what use such a thing could be. He famously replied, 'What is the use of a newborn baby?' Humanity had just learned to crawl through the air. 🎈
