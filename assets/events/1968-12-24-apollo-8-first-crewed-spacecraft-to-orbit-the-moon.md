In 1968, Apollo 8 took Frank Borman, Jim Lovell, and Bill Anders to the Moon. They didn't land, but they were the first humans to see the 'far side' with their own eyes. 

On Christmas Eve, they broadcasted a message back to Earth while orbiting the lunar surface. But the most enduring legacy was the 'Earthrise' photograph—a tiny, blue marble hanging in the blackness of space. It changed how we saw our home, making it look fragile, beautiful, and lonely all at once. 🌍
