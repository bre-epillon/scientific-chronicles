On the 20th anniversary of Gagarin's flight, NASA launched Columbia. Unlike previous spacecraft that were one-and-done, the Shuttle was designed to fly, land like a plane, and fly again. 

It was a technological marvel and the workhorse of the US space program for 30 years. It launched satellites, repaired Hubble, and built the ISS. The Shuttle made space feel almost routine—at least until we were reminded of how dangerous it really is. It was a bold attempt to make space travel sustainable. 🚀
