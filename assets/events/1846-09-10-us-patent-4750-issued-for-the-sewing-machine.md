Before Elias Howe, every piece of clothing was stitched by hand. Howe’s patent for the sewing machine featured the critical 'lockstitch' mechanism that we still use today. 

He struggled for years to sell his invention and had to sue other manufacturers (including Isaac Singer) for patent infringement. He eventually won and became very wealthy, but his machine did something even more important: it moved clothing production from the home to the factory, starting a revolution in fashion and labor. 🧵
