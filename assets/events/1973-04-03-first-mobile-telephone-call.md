On a sidewalk in New York City, Martin Cooper of Motorola pulled out a device the size of a brick and called Joel Engel at Bell Labs. His message? 'Joel, I'm calling you from a cellular phone, a real actual portable handheld cellular phone.' 

It was the ultimate power move. The 'brick' phone weighed over two pounds and had a battery life of about 20 minutes, but it proved that people wanted to talk to people, not to places. Thus began the era of walking into walls while checking your email. 📱
