On July 4, 2012, physicists at the Large Hadron Collider announced they'd found a particle that looked a lot like the Higgs Boson. 

This particle is the manifestation of the Higgs field, which is what gives other particles their mass. Without it, the universe would just be a bunch of weightless particles flying around at the speed of light. Finding it was the final piece of the Standard Model of particle physics. Peter Higgs, who predicted it in 1964, was in the audience and was seen wiping away a tear. Not bad for a day's work. 🎆
