In 1978, the birth of Louise Brown made headlines around the world. She was the first human born through in vitro fertilization (IVF), a process where an egg is fertilized by sperm outside the body. 

The event sparked a massive debate about the ethics of 'playing God,' but for millions of couples struggling with infertility, it was a message of hope. Today, millions of people have been born via IVF. Louise proved that science could help nature along in the most fundamental way possible. 👶
