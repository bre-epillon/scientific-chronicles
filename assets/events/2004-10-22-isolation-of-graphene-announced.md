In 2004, Andre Geim and Konstantin Novoselov managed to isolate a single layer of carbon atoms—graphene. How? They used a piece of adhesive tape to peel layers off a block of graphite until they had a sheet just one atom thick. 

Graphene is 200 times stronger than steel, a better conductor than copper, and almost transparent. It’s a 2D material with 3D possibilities. The duo won the Nobel Prize for their 'Friday Night Experiments,' proving that you don't always need a billion-dollar lab to change the world; sometimes, you just need some tape. 💎
