On June 21, 1948, the Small-Scale Experimental Machine, or 'Baby,' became the first computer in the world to execute a program stored in its own memory. 

Before this, 'programming' involved physical wires and switches. The Baby showed that a machine could store its own instructions, making it the direct ancestor of every laptop, server, and smartphone on Earth today. It only had 32 words of memory, but it was enough to start the digital revolution. 👶
