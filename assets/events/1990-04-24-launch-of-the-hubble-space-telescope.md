The Hubble Space Telescope was launched to see above the distortion of Earth's atmosphere. It started with a bit of a blurry vision problem (a tiny flaw in the mirror), but a 1993 repair mission gave it 'glasses.' 

Since then, Hubble has provided the most breathtaking images of the cosmos ever seen. It helped us determine the age of the universe, the existence of black holes at the center of galaxies, and the rate of cosmic expansion. It’s the most productive scientific instrument in history, and it's still hard at work. 🔭
