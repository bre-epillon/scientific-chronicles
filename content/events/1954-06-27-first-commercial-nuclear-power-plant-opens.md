In 1954, the Obninsk Nuclear Power Plant became the first in the world to generate electricity for a power grid. It was a small plant, but it proved that nuclear fission could be used for something other than bombs. 

It was the start of the 'Atoms for Peace' era, promising a future of power that was 'too cheap to meter.' While that didn't quite happen, nuclear energy remains a vital (and controversial) part of our carbon-free energy mix. It all started with this one little reactor in a town near Moscow. ⚛️
