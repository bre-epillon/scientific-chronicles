In 1998, Larry Page and Sergey Brin incorporated Google, a company dedicated to organizing the world's information. Their secret sauce was PageRank, an algorithm that ranked web pages based on how many other pages linked to them. 

Google quickly became the default way to navigate the internet. It was so effective that 'Googling' became a word. It turned the messy web into a searchable library, and eventually, it turned itself into a tech empire that knows what you want before you even finish typing. 🔍
