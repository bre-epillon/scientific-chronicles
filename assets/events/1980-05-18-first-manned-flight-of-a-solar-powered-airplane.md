Designed by the legendary Paul MacCready, the Gossamer Penguin was a lightweight aircraft powered entirely by solar cells. On its first flight, it carried MacCready's 13-year-old son, Marshall. 

It wasn't a fast plane, but it proved that renewable energy could be used for flight. It paved the way for the Solar Impulse, which eventually flew around the world. It showed that the future of aviation doesn't have to be loud or dirty; it can be as quiet as a sunbeam. ☀️
