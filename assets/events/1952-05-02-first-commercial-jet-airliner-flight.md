In 1952, the British-built de Havilland Comet flew from London to Johannesburg, ushering in the Jet Age. Jet engines were faster, smoother, and quieter than the old propeller engines. 

While the Comet eventually suffered from tragic structural failures (due to the then-unknown problem of metal fatigue), it set the standard for modern air travel. It showed that we could cross continents in hours instead of days. The world was shrinking, and the vacation was never the same again. ✈️
