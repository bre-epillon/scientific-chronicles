The Xerox Alto was years ahead of its time. It had a graphical user interface, windows, icons, and—for the first time as a standard feature—a mouse. 

It was the computer that inspired Steve Jobs and Bill Gates to create the Macintosh and Windows. Xerox didn't know what they had and never really marketed it to the public, but the Alto was the DNA of every computer we use today. It was the future, delivered a decade early. 🖱️
