The Kyoto Protocol was the first international treaty that committed countries to reducing greenhouse gas emissions. It was a recognition that global warming was a real and present danger caused by human activity. 

While it was criticized for not going far enough and for the fact that some major polluters didn't join, it set the stage for all future climate negotiations. It was the moment the world admitted we have a carbon problem. Admitting you have a problem is the first step, right? 🌡️
