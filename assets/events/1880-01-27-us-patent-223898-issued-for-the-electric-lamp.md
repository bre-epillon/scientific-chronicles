After thousands of failed attempts to find the perfect filament, Thomas Edison finally patented his carbon-filament incandescent lamp. 

While he wasn't the first to dream of electric light, he was the first to make it commercially viable and long-lasting. This patent was the cornerstone of the Edison Electric Light Company, which eventually became General Electric. Edison proved that genius is indeed 1% inspiration and 99% not letting your patent expire. 💡
