At Promontory Summit, Utah, the Union Pacific and Central Pacific railroads finally met. A golden spike was driven into the ground, and a telegraph message was sent to the whole country: 'DONE.' 

Before the railroad, a trip across America took six months by wagon. After, it took one week. It changed everything—from how we moved goods to how we thought about time. The frontier was officially closed, and the modern United States was born. 🚂
