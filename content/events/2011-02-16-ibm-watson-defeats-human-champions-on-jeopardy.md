Jeopardy! is hard for computers because it involves wordplay, irony, and obscure cultural references. But in 2011, IBM's Watson took on all-time champions Ken Jennings and Brad Rutter and crushed them. 

Watson wasn't connected to the internet; it had millions of pages of documents stored in its memory and used natural language processing to understand the clues. Ken Jennings famously wrote on his final podium: 'I for one welcome our new computer overlords.' It was a brilliant demonstration of how far AI had come in understanding human language. 🧠
