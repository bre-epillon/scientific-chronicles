On a chilly March night in 1781, William Herschel was scanning the skies with a telescope he built himself (talk about a DIY project). He spotted something that didn't look like a star. At first, he thought it was a comet, because obviously, there were only six planets. 

Turns out, it was the first planet discovered since antiquity. Herschel wanted to name it 'Georgium Sidus' after King George III, but the rest of the world wisely decided that naming a planet after a British monarch was a bit much. They settled on Uranus, ensuring that middle schoolers would have a joke to tell for the next few centuries. 🔭
