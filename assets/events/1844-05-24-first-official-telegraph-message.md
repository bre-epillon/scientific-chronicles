On May 24, 1844, Samuel Morse sat in the US Capitol and tapped out a series of dots and dashes. Seconds later, his partner in Baltimore received the message: 'What hath God wrought.' 

It was the first long-distance electronic communication in history. Before the telegraph, information traveled at the speed of a horse. Suddenly, it traveled at the speed of light. The world started getting smaller, and the 'Information Age' took its first, clicky breaths. 📻
