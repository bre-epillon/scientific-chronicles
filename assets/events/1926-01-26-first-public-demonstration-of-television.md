In a small lab in London, John Logie Baird demonstrated the first working television system to a group of scientists. The image was tiny, flickering, and in shades of gray, but it was unmistakably a human face. 

Baird’s mechanical television was eventually replaced by electronic systems, but he was the first to prove that you could transmit moving images through the air. He turned every living room into a theater and gave the world a whole new way to ignore each other. 📺
