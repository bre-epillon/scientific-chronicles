Claude Shannon’s 1948 paper is the reason we measure things in megabits and gigabytes. He showed that all communication could be boiled down to 0s and 1s. 

Shannon defined how to measure information, how to compress it, and how to transmit it reliably through noise. He effectively founded Information Theory, turning communication into a branch of mathematics.
