8 August 1902 is the anniversary of the birth of Paul Adrien Maurice Dirac, 
English theoretical physicist regarded as (one of) the most infuential physicists of the 20th century.
Dirac made fundamental contributions to the early development of both quantum mechanics and quantum electrodynamics. 
Among other discoveries, he formulated the Dirac equation which describes the behaviour of fermions and predicted 
the existence of antimatter. Dirac shared the 1933 Nobel Prize in Physics with Erwin Schr—dinger 
"for the discovery of new productive forms of atomic theory". He also made significant contributions to the 
reconciliation of the theory of relativity with quantum mechanics.
