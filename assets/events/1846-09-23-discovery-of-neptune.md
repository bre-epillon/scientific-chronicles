By the 1840s, astronomers noticed that Uranus wasn't following its predicted orbit. Urbain Le Verrier used math to predict that another, unseen planet must be pulling on it. 

He sent his calculations to Johann Galle, who looked exactly where Le Verrier told him to and found Neptune within an hour. It was a stunning triumph for Newtonian physics—proving that math can literally show us parts of the universe we haven't seen yet. Neptune: the first planet discovered by calculation. 🌌
