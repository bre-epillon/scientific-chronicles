Todat is the birhday of Salvador Edward Luria (1912-1991) was an Italian microbiologist, 
later a naturalized U.S. citizen. 
He won the Nobel Prize in Medicine in 1969 for the discoveries on the replication mechanism and the genetic structure of viruses.
Salvador Luria also showed that bacterial resistance to viruses (phages) is genetically inherited. 
After graduating from the University of Turin in 1935, where he met Rita-Levi Montalcini annd Renato Dulbecco, he moved to University 
of Rome where he became friend of Enrico Fermi.
From 1938 stayed in Paris, until the nazi invaded France. He crossed the whole France with a bike and flied to the USA from Marseille. 
After spending 3 years at Columbia University, he went to Indiana University, where he supervised the PhD work of James D. Whatson, 
the co-discoverer of the structure of DNA.
