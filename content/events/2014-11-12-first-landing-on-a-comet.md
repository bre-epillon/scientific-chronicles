The European Space Agency's Rosetta mission achieved the impossible: it caught up with Comet 67P and sent the Philae lander down to its surface. 

Philae’s harpoons didn't fire, so it bounced twice and ended up in a dark crack where its solar panels couldn't reach the sun. But it still managed to send back incredible data before its batteries ran out. It found organic molecules, the building blocks of life, on a comet. It proved that catching a comet is like trying to land on a fast-moving, icy mountain in the dark. ☄️
