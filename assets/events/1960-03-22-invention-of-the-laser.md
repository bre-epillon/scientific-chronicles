In 1960, a patent was granted for an 'Optical Maser'—now known as the laser. Charles Townes and Arthur Schawlow figured out how to create a beam of light that is perfectly coherent and focused. 

At first, people called the laser 'a solution looking for a problem.' Today, we use them for everything: scanning groceries, surgery, cutting steel, and playing DVDs (back when we still did that). It’s a fundamental tool of the modern world, proving that sometimes you just need a very focused light to see the way forward. 🔦
