The Magellan-Elcano expedition set out with five ships and 270 men. Three years later, only the *Victoria* returned, carrying 18 survivors. Magellan himself had died in the Philippines. 

It was the first time humans had sailed all the way around the world. It fundamentally changed our understanding of the planet's size and the layout of the oceans. It was the 16th-century equivalent of the Moon landing—a brutal, dangerous, and world-altering journey. 🌍
