In 1940, four boys in France followed their dog, Robot, into a hole left by an uprooted tree. They found themselves in a cave covered in stunning paintings of horses, deer, and bulls from 17,000 years ago. 

Lascaux is one of the most important sites of prehistoric art in the world. It showed that our ancestors weren't just struggling to survive; they were artists with a deep sense of beauty and storytelling. The cave is now closed to the public to protect the art, but its impact remains. 🐂
