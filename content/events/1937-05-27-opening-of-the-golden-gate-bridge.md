Built over a strait known for high winds, fog, and deep water, the Golden Gate Bridge was considered 'the bridge that couldn't be built.' 

Engineer Joseph Strauss insisted on safety nets, which saved the lives of 19 workers (the 'Halfway to Hell Club'). The bridge was painted 'International Orange' to make it visible in the fog, and it quickly became one of the most photographed structures in the world. It’s a graceful span that makes a dangerous crossing look easy. 🌉
