📌 August 2, 1932.

On this day the first experimental evidence of the existence of the positron was recovered by Carl David Anderson, an American physicist, eventually winner of the Nobel Prize in 1936.

The discover was simultaneously one of the first and most important confirmation of the Dirac's effort of unifying two apparently disjoint theories: quantum mechanics and special relativity.

The discovery was extremely important even because in 1930 was still believed that all the Universe was made only by 4 fundamental particles: neutrons, protons, electrons and photons. 
After the discovery of Anderson, instead, many new fundamental particles were discovered. In these regards, Anderson posed then the first milestone in the development of the Standard Model.
