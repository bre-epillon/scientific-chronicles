As Voyager 1 was leaving the solar system, Carl Sagan convinced NASA to turn the camera around for one last look. From 3.7 billion miles away, the Earth appeared as a single pixel of light caught in a sunbeam. 

Sagan’s subsequent reflection on the 'pale blue dot' became a legendary call for humility and stewardship. He reminded us that 'on it, everyone you love, everyone you know, everyone you ever heard of, lived out their lives.' It’s the ultimate lesson in perspective. 🌌
