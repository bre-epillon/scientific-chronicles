Alexei Leonov became the first person to leave his spacecraft and float in open space. He was tethered by a 17-foot cable and spent 12 minutes outside Voskhod 2. 

It wasn't all smooth sailing; his spacesuit puffed up in the vacuum so much that he couldn't get back through the airlock. He had to manually bleed some oxygen out of his suit to shrink enough to fit back inside. Talk about a high-stress exit strategy. He proved that humans could work outside their ships, a necessity for everything from repairs to Moon landings. 👨‍🚀
