In the bright orange Bell X-1, nicknamed 'Glamorous Glennis,' Chuck Yeager became the first human to fly faster than the speed of sound. 

Scientists weren't sure if the plane would disintegrate when it hit the 'sound barrier.' Instead, there was just a loud boom and a smooth ride. Yeager had broken his ribs in a horse-riding accident two days before, so he had to use a sawed-off broom handle to close the hatch. It was the dawn of the supersonic age, where we learned to outrun our own noise. ✈️
