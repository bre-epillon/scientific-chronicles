In 1971, Intel released the 4004, the world's first commercially available microprocessor. It contained 2,300 transistors and had as much power as the ENIAC computer from 1946 (which took up an entire room). 

This 'computer on a chip' changed everything. It meant that you could put computing power into anything—calculators, cars, washing machines, and eventually, the PC. It was the moment that technology became small enough to be everywhere. 💾
