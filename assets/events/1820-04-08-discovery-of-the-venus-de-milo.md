In 1820, a farmer named Yorgos Kentrotas was looking for stones to build a wall on the island of Milos when he found a buried statue of Aphrodite. 

She was beautiful, classical, and missing her arms. The French eventually bought her and put her in the Louvre, where she became a global icon of beauty. To this day, people are still debating what she was doing with those missing arms—holding an apple, a mirror, or maybe just shrugging? We’ll never know. 🏛️
