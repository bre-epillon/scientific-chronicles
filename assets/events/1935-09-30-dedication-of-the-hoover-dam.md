In the middle of the Great Depression, thousands of workers built a dam so large it could hold back the entire flow of the Colorado River for two years. 

It was a triumph of 1930s engineering, using enough concrete to pave a road from New York to San Francisco. It provided power and water to the Southwest, allowing cities like Las Vegas and Los Angeles to bloom in the desert. It’s a massive, curved monument to what we can achieve when we're desperate and determined. 🌊
