The first message ever sent between two computers on ARPANET (the precursor to the internet) was supposed to be 'LOGIN.' 

Charley Kline tried to send the 'L,' which worked. He sent the 'O,' which worked. Then the system crashed before he could send the 'G.' So the internet's first official communication was just 'LO'—the universe's most accidental 'Hello.' They got it working an hour later, but the humble beginning is a reminder that even the most revolutionary tech starts with a glitch. 💻
