Cyrus Field had a crazy idea: run a copper wire 2,500 miles across the floor of the Atlantic Ocean. After several failed attempts and lost cables, he finally succeeded in 1858. 

Queen Victoria sent a message of greeting to President James Buchanan, and it only took 17 hours to arrive! (Which was way better than the 10 days it took by ship). The cable only lasted three weeks before it failed, but the proof of concept was there. The global village was born, even if it was a bit glitchy at first. 🌊
