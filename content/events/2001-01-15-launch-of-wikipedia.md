In 2001, Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger launched Wikipedia as a side project to their more traditional (and failed) encyclopedia, Nupedia. 

The idea that 'anyone can edit' sounded like a recipe for disaster. Instead, it created the largest, most comprehensive collection of knowledge in human history. Wikipedia proved that the 'wisdom of the crowd' is a powerful thing. Today, it’s the first stop for almost every question we have, kept alive by a global army of volunteers. 📚
