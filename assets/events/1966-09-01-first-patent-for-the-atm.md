Luther Simjian had the idea for a 'Bankograph'—a machine that could accept deposits and dispense cash. He convinced a bank in New York to trial it in 1960, but it was a flop. 

According to Simjian, 'The only people using the machines were prostitutes and gamblers who didn't want to face tellers.' He was ahead of his time. It took another few years for the idea to catch on, but today, we can't imagine life without the 24/7 convenience of the ATM. Simjian: the man who made 'banker's hours' obsolete. 🏧
