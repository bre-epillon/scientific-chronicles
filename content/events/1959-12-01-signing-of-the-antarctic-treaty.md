In the middle of the Cold War, twelve countries signed a remarkable agreement: they would set aside the entire continent of Antarctica for peaceful, scientific purposes only. 

They banned military activity, mining, and nuclear testing. It was the first arms control agreement of the era and remains one of the most successful international treaties ever signed. Antarctica is now a giant, frozen laboratory where countries that are enemies elsewhere work together to study the Earth's history. It’s a cool place for a warm idea. ❄️
