Alexander Fleming wasn't trying to change the world in 1928; he was just a bit messy. He went on vacation and left some petri dishes out. When he came back, he found a mold called *Penicillium notatum* had started killing his bacteria cultures. 

Instead of throwing it away, he took a closer look. He'd found the world's first true antibiotic. It took a few more years and some help from other scientists to make it a medicine, but Fleming's 'mold juice' has since saved millions of lives. Cleanliness is next to godliness, but messiness is occasionally next to genius. 🧫
