In 1974, paleoanthropologists found a skeleton that was 40% complete—a rarity for such an old fossil. They named her Lucy (after the Beatles song 'Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds,' which was playing in their camp). 

Lucy was an *Australopithecus afarensis*, a species that walked upright like us but still had an ape-like face. She was the 'missing link' that proved our ancestors were walking on two legs long before they had big brains. She's the great-great-great...-great grandmother of us all. 🦴
