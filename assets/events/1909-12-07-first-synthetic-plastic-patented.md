Leo Baekeland was looking for a replacement for shellac when he created Bakelite, the world's first fully synthetic plastic. It was heat-resistant, didn't conduct electricity, and could be molded into any shape. 

Bakelite was used for everything from telephones to jewelry to engine parts. It ushered in the 'Age of Plastics,' for better and for worse. Baekeland showed that we didn't have to rely on nature for materials; we could create our own. It was the birth of the synthetic world. 🧪
