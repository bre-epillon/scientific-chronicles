Critics said it was impossible because the Earth is curved and radio waves travel in straight lines. But Guglielmo Marconi didn't listen. On December 12, 1901, he sat in Newfoundland and heard three faint clicks: the letter 'S' in Morse code sent from England. 

It turns out the ionosphere reflects radio waves, allowing them to bounce around the curve of the Earth. Marconi had bridged the ocean with nothing but air. Wireless communication was no longer a dream; it was the new reality. 📻
