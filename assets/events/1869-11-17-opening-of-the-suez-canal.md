For centuries, ships going from Europe to Asia had to sail all the way around Africa. In 1869, the Suez Canal changed that by cutting through 120 miles of Egyptian desert. 

It was a massive engineering feat that fundamentally changed global trade. Suddenly, the trip was 4,300 miles shorter. It made the world smaller and established Egypt as a vital crossroads for the entire planet. It’s the ultimate shortcut. 🚢
