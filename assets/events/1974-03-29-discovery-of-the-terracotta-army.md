In 1974, farmers in Xi'an were digging a well when they found some clay fragments. They had stumbled upon the tomb of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China. 

Buried with him was an entire army of 8,000 life-sized terracotta soldiers, each with a unique face, along with horses and chariots. They were meant to protect the emperor in the afterlife. It is one of the most stunning archaeological finds in history, a massive, silent monument to power and craftsmanship. 🏹
