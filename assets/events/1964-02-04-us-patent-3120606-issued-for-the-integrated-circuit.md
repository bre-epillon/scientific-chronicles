In 1958, Jack Kilby at Texas Instruments had a simple idea: if all the components of a circuit could be made of the same material, they could be placed on a single chip. 

He built the first one out of germanium, and in 1964, he finally got the patent for the 'Miniaturized Electronic Circuits.' Along with Robert Noyce's work at Fairchild, this led to the microchips that power everything today. It was the end of the 'tyranny of numbers' and the start of the silicon era. 💾
