In 1899, Sigmund Freud introduced the world to the unconscious mind. He argued that dreams are 'the royal road to the knowledge of the unconscious' and that they represent fulfilled (often repressed) wishes. 

While many of Freud's specific theories have been debunked or refined, his idea that our minds have hidden depths that we aren't aware of changed everything from psychology to literature. Suddenly, a cigar wasn't always just a cigar. Freud made us realize that the most interesting things in the room are the ones we aren't talking about. 🛌
