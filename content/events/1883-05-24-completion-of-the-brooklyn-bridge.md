The Brooklyn Bridge was the longest suspension bridge in the world when it opened. It was a family affair: John Roebling designed it, his son Washington built it (while suffering from the 'bends'), and his daughter-in-law Emily managed the project when Washington got too sick to leave the house. 

On opening day, people were so worried it would collapse that P.T. Barnum eventually led 21 elephants across it to prove it was safe. It remains one of the most beautiful and enduring symbols of New York City. 🌉
