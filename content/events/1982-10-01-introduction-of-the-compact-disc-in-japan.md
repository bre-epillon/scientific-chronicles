In October 1982, the first commercial CDs went on sale in Japan, along with the first player, the Sony CDP-101. The first album released was Billy Joel's *52nd Street*. 

CDs used lasers to read digital data, providing clear sound that didn't hiss or pop. They were supposedly 'indestructible' (which we later found out was a bit of an exaggeration), and they changed how we consumed music. The CD paved the way for the digital audio revolution that would eventually lead to MP3s and streaming. 💿
