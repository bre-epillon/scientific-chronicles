While everyone was looking up at the stars, Jacques Piccard and Don Walsh went the other way. In the bathyscaphe *Trieste*, they dove into the Challenger Deep, the deepest point in the Mariana Trench. 

At 35,800 feet down, the pressure was over 1,000 times that at the surface—enough to crush a regular submarine like a soda can. They stayed for 20 minutes, saw a flatfish (proving life exists even there), and came back up. It remains one of the most extreme journeys in human history. 🌊
