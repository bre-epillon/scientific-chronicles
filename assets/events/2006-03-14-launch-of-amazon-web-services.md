In 2006, Amazon launched S3 and EC2, allowing developers to rent computing power and storage over the internet. 

It was the birth of 'cloud computing.' Suddenly, a startup didn't need its own server room; it could just use Amazon's. AWS has since become the backbone of the modern internet, powering everything from Netflix to NASA. It turned computing into a utility, like water or electricity. The cloud is just someone else's computer, but Amazon has a lot of them. ☁️
