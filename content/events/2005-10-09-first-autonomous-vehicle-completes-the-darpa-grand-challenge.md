In 2004, not a single car finished the DARPA Grand Challenge. In 2005, Stanford's Stanley drove 132 miles across the Mojave Desert in under seven hours with no human help. 

Stanley used LIDAR, cameras, and GPS to navigate the rugged terrain. It proved that self-driving cars weren't just a fantasy; they were an engineering problem that could be solved. It kicked off the massive industry of autonomous vehicles that is now testing on our city streets. The car of the future finally learned how to drive. 🚗
