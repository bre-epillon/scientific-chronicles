In 1919, Ernest Rutherford showed that when you fire alpha particles at nitrogen gas, you get oxygen and... something else. That 'something else' was a hydrogen nucleus, which he realized was a fundamental particle found in all atoms. 

He called it the proton (from the Greek for 'first'). It was the first time someone had deliberately changed one element into another—the dream of the alchemists finally came true, just with more math and fewer potions. Rutherford had found the positive core of our world. ➕
