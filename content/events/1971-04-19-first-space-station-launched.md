In 1971, the USSR launched Salyut 1, the world's first space station. It wasn't just a capsule; it was a laboratory where cosmonauts could live and work for weeks at a time. 

It paved the way for Skylab, Mir, and eventually the International Space Station. Salyut 1 showed that space could be more than just a destination for a quick visit; it could be a place where humans actually reside. Living in a metal tube in a vacuum: the ultimate test of roommate compatibility. 🛰️
