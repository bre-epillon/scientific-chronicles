Phineas Gage was a responsible, well-liked foreman until a blasting accident sent a three-foot iron rod through his skull. Miraculously, he survived, but he was 'no longer Gage.' 

He became fitful, irreverent, and impulsive. This tragic case study proved to doctors that the brain's frontal lobe is responsible for personality and social behavior. Gage’s misfortune was the first clear evidence that our 'self' is physically located in our gray matter. He lived for another 12 years, carrying the rod with him as a macabre souvenir. 🧠
