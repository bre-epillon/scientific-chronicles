In Cape Town, South Africa, Dr. Christiaan Barnard did something that sounded like science fiction: he took a heart from a deceased donor and put it into a living man named Louis Washkansky. 

Washkansky lived for only 18 days after the surgery, but he proved that the human heart was just another organ that could be replaced. The event was a global sensation and pushed the boundaries of what medicine—and ethics—could handle. It was a literal change of heart for the medical community. ❤️
