In the early 1950s, polio was the most feared disease in America, paralyzing thousands of children every year. When Jonas Salk’s vaccine was announced as safe and effective on April 12, 1955, people literally danced in the streets. 

When asked who owned the patent on the vaccine, Salk famously replied, 'The people, I would say. There is no patent. Could you patent the sun?' He chose humanity over profit, and today, polio is nearly a thing of the past. A true superhero without a cape. 💉
