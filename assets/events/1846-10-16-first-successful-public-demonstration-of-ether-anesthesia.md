Before 1846, surgery was a terrifying, painful ordeal that required strong men to hold the patient down. But at Massachusetts General Hospital, William T.G. Morton used sulfuric ether to put a patient into a deep, painless sleep. 

After the operation, the surgeon reportedly turned to the audience and said, 'Gentlemen, this is no humbug.' It was the birth of modern anesthesia. Suddenly, doctors could take their time and perform complex, life-saving procedures without the patient screaming. It was the greatest gift to surgery since the knife. 💤
