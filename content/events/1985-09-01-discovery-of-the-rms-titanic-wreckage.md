After 73 years in the dark, the Titanic was finally found by a joint French-American expedition. Robert Ballard used a deep-sea robot to spot one of the ship's boilers 12,500 feet down. 

The wreck was split in two and surrounded by a vast debris field of shoes, suitcases, and chandeliers. Finding it was a technological triumph, but seeing the silent ruins was a somber reminder of the 1,500 lives lost. The ship that 'couldn't sink' finally had its final resting place mapped. 🚢
