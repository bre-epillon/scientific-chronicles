Roy Plunkett was trying to make a new refrigerant when he opened a tank of gas and found a white, slippery powder instead. He had accidentally created polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), now known as Teflon. 

It is one of the slipperiest substances known to man and is incredibly resistant to heat and chemicals. It was used in the Manhattan Project, then on frying pans, and now in everything from spacesuits to heart valves. Plunkett’s accident made the world a much less sticky place. 🍳
