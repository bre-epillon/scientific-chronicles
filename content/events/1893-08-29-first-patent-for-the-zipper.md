In 1893, Whitcomb Judson patented a 'Clasp Locker or Unlocker for Shoes.' It was complicated, expensive, and didn't really work very well. 

It took another twenty years and several more inventors to turn Judson’s idea into the smooth, reliable zipper we know today. It’s a classic example of how a great idea can take decades to become a practical reality. Today, we take the zipper for granted, but it was once a high-tech marvel of precision engineering. 🤐
