In 1997, the reigning world chess champion Garry Kasparov was defeated by IBM's Deep Blue in a six-game match. It was the first time a computer had beaten a world champion in a tournament setting. 

Kasparov was visibly frustrated, but the win showed that brute-force computing power could solve complex problems that were previously thought to require human 'intuition.' It was a landmark moment for AI, even if Deep Blue only knew how to do one thing: play chess. ♟️
