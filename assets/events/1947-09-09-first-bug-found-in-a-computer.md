While working on the Harvard Mark II computer, Grace Hopper and her team found a glitch. They traced it to a relay where a moth had gotten stuck, shorting out the circuit. 

They taped the moth into their logbook with the note: 'First actual case of bug being found.' While the term 'bug' had been used by engineers before, this was the first time it was literally true. Grace Hopper went on to become a pioneer of programming, but she'll always be remembered for the moth. 🦋
