On March 7, 1876, the US Patent Office granted Alexander Graham Bell patent number 174,465 for an 'improvement in telegraphy'—which we now know as the telephone. 

Bell beat Elisha Gray to the patent office by just a few hours. The patent led to decades of legal battles, but it established the foundation for the telecommunications industry. Bell’s vision was a world where people could speak across distances, though he probably didn't foresee the invention of the 'spam call.' ☎️
