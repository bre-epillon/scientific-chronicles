In 1961, the first Unimate robot was installed at a General Motors plant in New Jersey. Its job was to pick up white-hot pieces of die-cast metal and stack them. 

It was a job too dangerous and boring for humans, which is exactly why robots are so useful. Unimate was essentially a giant mechanical arm controlled by a magnetic drum. It didn't look like C-3PO, but it was the start of the automation revolution that would transform manufacturing across the globe. The age of the robot had truly begun. 🤖
