In the wake of WWII, Stanley Milgram wanted to understand how people could participate in the Holocaust. He set up an experiment where 'teachers' were told to give increasingly painful electric shocks to 'learners' (who were actually actors). 

The result was chilling: a majority of people were willing to deliver what they thought were lethal shocks just because an authority figure told them the experiment required it. It revealed the terrifying power of obedience and changed how we think about ethics and human nature. It’s a study that still makes us uncomfortable today. ⚡
