During Napoleon's campaign in Egypt, a soldier named Pierre-François Bouchard found a black stone slab covered in writing. It had the same message in three different scripts: Ancient Greek, Demotic, and Egyptian Hieroglyphs. 

Because we could already read the Greek, the stone became the 'decoder ring' that allowed Jean-François Champollion to finally crack the code of hieroglyphs. It opened up 3,000 years of Egyptian history that had been silent for millennia. It’s the original translation app. 🗿
