In 1932, Carl Anderson was looking at cosmic rays and saw something weird: a particle with the mass of an electron but a positive charge. It wasn't a mistake; it was the positron, the first piece of antimatter ever found. 

This confirmed Paul Dirac's wild mathematical prediction that every particle has an antiparticle. It sounds like science fiction, but it's why we have PET scans today. Just don't let a positron shake hands with an electron unless you want a tiny explosion of pure energy. 🎇
