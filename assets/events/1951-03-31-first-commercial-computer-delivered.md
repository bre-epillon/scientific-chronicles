The UNIVAC I was the first general-purpose electronic digital computer designed for business and administrative use. The first one was delivered to the US Census Bureau in 1951. 

It was a massive machine that used 5,000 vacuum tubes and could perform 1,000 calculations per second. It became famous in 1952 when it accurately predicted the outcome of the presidential election on live TV with only 1% of the vote in. Suddenly, the world realized that computers weren't just for math—they were for everything. 🖥️
