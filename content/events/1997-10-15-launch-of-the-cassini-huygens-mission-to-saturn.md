Cassini-Huygens was a massive, ambitious mission. It spent 13 years orbiting Saturn, revealing liquid methane lakes on Titan, icy geysers on Enceladus, and the incredible complexity of the rings. 

The Huygens probe actually landed on Titan, giving us our first look at a surface that looked eerily like Earth—if Earth were made of frozen gas. Cassini's mission ended in 2017 with a deliberate plunge into Saturn's atmosphere to protect the moons from contamination. It went out in a blaze of scientific glory. 🪐
