Built for the 1889 World's Fair, the Eiffel Tower was meant to be temporary. Critics called it a 'truly tragic street lamp' and a 'giant smokestack.' 

But Gustave Eiffel knew his tower was a masterpiece of wind engineering and structural design. It was the tallest building in the world for 41 years. Paris eventually fell in love with it, and it became the most visited paid monument in the world. Sometimes, the 'eyesore' of today is the icon of tomorrow. 🗼
