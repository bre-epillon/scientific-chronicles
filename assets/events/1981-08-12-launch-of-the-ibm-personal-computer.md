When IBM decided to enter the personal computer market, they did it by breaking their own rules. They used off-the-shelf parts and an operating system from a tiny company called Microsoft. 

The IBM PC became the industry standard, creating the 'IBM Compatible' market that dominated computing for decades. It brought the PC into the mainstream office and solidified the 'Wintel' partnership. Big Blue had arrived, and the computer was no longer just a toy for geeks. 🖥️
