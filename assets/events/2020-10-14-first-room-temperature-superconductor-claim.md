In 2020, researchers claimed to have found a material that could conduct electricity without any resistance at room temperature. 

There was a catch: it required the pressure of 2.6 million atmospheres (about the same as at the center of the Earth). While the original paper was later retracted and the field remains highly controversial, the event sparked a massive surge of interest in finding a practical room-temperature superconductor. If we ever find one that works at normal pressure, it would revolutionize everything from the power grid to levitating trains. 🧲
