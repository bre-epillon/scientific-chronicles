By June 1944, American chemical companies had figured out how to mass-produce penicillin using deep-tank fermentation (and a particularly moldy cantaloupe from a market in Peoria). 

There was enough of the drug ready for the D-Day invasion to treat every soldier who needed it. Before penicillin, more soldiers died of infection than from combat wounds. The mass production of this antibiotic was as important to the war effort as the planes and tanks, and it changed medicine forever after the war. 💊
