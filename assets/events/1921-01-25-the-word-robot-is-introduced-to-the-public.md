---

In Karel Čapek's play *R.U.R.* (Rossum's Universal Robots), the word 'robot' was used for the first time. It comes from the Czech word 'robota,' meaning forced labor. 

In the play, the robots aren't mechanical; they're biological beings designed to do all the world's work. Of course, they eventually revolt and wipe out humanity (because that’s how sci-fi works). While the word is now used for metal machines, it started as a social commentary on labor and technology. We've been worrying about our creations taking over for over a century now. 🤖
