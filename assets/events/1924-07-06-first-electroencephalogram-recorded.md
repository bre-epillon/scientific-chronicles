Hans Berger was obsessed with the idea of telepathy. While he didn't find a way to read minds, he did find a way to record the brain's electrical activity. 

In 1924, he recorded the first human EEG, identifying the 'alpha' and 'beta' waves that change depending on whether we're awake, asleep, or focused. It was the first time we could 'see' the brain thinking without opening the skull. It turned neuroscience into a hard science and gave us a vital tool for studying sleep and epilepsy. 🧠
