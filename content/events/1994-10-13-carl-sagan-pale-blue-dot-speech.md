In a speech at Cornell University, Carl Sagan reflected on the photograph of Earth taken by Voyager 1 from the edge of the solar system. He famously described our home as a 'mote of dust suspended in a sunbeam.' 

His words were a powerful call for compassion and environmental stewardship. He argued that our tiny, lonely planet is the only home we've ever known, and it's our responsibility to cherish and preserve it. It remains one of the most moving pieces of scientific communication ever delivered. Perspective: it’s a heck of a thing. 🌍
