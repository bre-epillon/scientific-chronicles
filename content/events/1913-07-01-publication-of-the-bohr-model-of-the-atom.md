In 1913, Niels Bohr published his model of the atom. He proposed that electrons travel in fixed orbits around the nucleus, like planets around a sun, and can only jump between those orbits by absorbing or emitting energy. 

It was a brilliant blend of classical physics and the new quantum theory. While we've since moved on to more complex 'cloud' models, the Bohr atom is still the one you see on every science textbook and nuclear logo. It made the invisible world of the atom feel structured and understandable. ⚛️
