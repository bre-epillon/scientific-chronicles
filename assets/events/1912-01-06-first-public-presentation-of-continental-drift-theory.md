In 1912, Alfred Wegener looked at a map and noticed that South America and Africa looked like they fit together. He proposed that all the continents were once a single landmass called Pangaea. 

Other scientists laughed at him. They asked, 'How do you move a continent?' Wegener didn't have the answer (plate tectonics wasn't discovered yet), so he died before his theory was accepted. Today, we know he was right. The ground beneath your feet is on a very slow, multi-million-year road trip. 🌍
