For centuries, maps included a hypothetical 'Terra Australis.' In 1820, Fabian Gottlieb von Bellingshausen finally saw it—a massive ice shelf attached to land. 

He had found the seventh continent. It was a desolate, frozen world that would remain largely unexplored for another century. His sighting kicked off an era of heroic (and often doomed) exploration to the bottom of the world. Antarctica: the last place on Earth to be found. ❄️
