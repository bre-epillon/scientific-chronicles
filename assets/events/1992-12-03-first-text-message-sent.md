On December 3, 1992, engineer Neil Papworth sat at a computer and sent a message to Richard Jarvis's mobile phone (a hefty Orbitel 901). The message read: 'Merry Christmas.' 

Jarvis couldn't reply because phones didn't have keyboards yet, but the Short Message Service (SMS) had been born. It changed how we communicate, leading to the rise of 'txt spk,' emojis, and the anxiety of seeing those three little dots while someone types. 📱
