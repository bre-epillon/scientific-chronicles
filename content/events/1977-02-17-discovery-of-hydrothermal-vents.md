In 1977, the submersible *Alvin* was exploring a ridge near the Galápagos Islands when it found something impossible: giant tube worms and blind shrimp living around volcanic vents in the pitch-black deep sea. 

This discovery proved that life doesn't always need photosynthesis. Instead of the sun, these ecosystems run on 'chemosynthesis,' using chemicals from the Earth's crust. It changed our understanding of where life can exist, both on Earth and on other icy moons in our solar system. The deep ocean is a lot more alive than we thought. 🦀
