In 1997, the Mars Pathfinder used a revolutionary 'bouncing' landing system to reach the Red Planet. Inside was Sojourner, the first successful rover on Mars. 

Sojourner was about the size of a microwave oven, but it captured the world's imagination. It rolled around for 83 days, taking pictures of rocks with names like 'Barnacle Bill' and 'Yogi.' It proved that rovers were the future of planetary exploration. Every giant Mars rover since then owes a debt to this little pioneer. 🤖
