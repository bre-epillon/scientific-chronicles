In Larderello, Italy, engineers built the first plant to turn the Earth's internal heat into electricity. They used steam rising from volcanic vents to spin a turbine. 

It was a brilliant way to use a natural resource that had been scaring people for centuries. Today, geothermal energy is a key part of the renewable energy transition, especially in places like Iceland. It turns out the ground under our feet is a giant, sustainable battery. 🌋
