In 1826 (or maybe 1827), Nicéphore Niépce used a process called 'heliography' to capture the view from his window in France. He used a plate coated in bitumen, which hardened when exposed to light. 

The exposure took at least eight hours, which is why the sun seems to be hitting both sides of the buildings. It was a grainy, fuzzy image, but it was the first time a moment had been permanently 'frozen' by light. It was the birth of our visual history. Say cheese! 📸
