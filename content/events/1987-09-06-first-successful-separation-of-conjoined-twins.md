---

In a 22-hour marathon surgery, Dr. Ben Carson and his team at Johns Hopkins separated seven-month-old twins who were joined at the back of the head. 

It was a high-stakes procedure that involved stopping the boys' hearts and cooling their bodies to prevent brain damage. Both boys survived the surgery, a feat that had never been achieved before for twins joined in that way. It pushed the boundaries of pediatric neurosurgery and showed what was possible with incredible precision and teamwork. 🏥
