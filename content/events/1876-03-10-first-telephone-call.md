Three days after getting his patent, Alexander Graham Bell was testing his new invention when he spilled some battery acid on his pants. He shouted into the transmitter: 'Mr. Watson, come here, I want to see you!' 

Thomas Watson heard him clearly through the wire in the next room. It was the first time human speech had been transmitted electrically. Bell had just made the world a lot louder and more connected. Little did he know he'd eventually be responsible for millions of 'can you hear me now?' moments. ☎️
