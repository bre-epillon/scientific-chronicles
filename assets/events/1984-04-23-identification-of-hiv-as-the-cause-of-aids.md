In the early 1980s, a new disease was devastating communities, and no one knew why. On April 23, 1984, the US government announced that researchers had identified the virus responsible: HIV. 

The discovery was the result of a heated race between American Robert Gallo and Frenchman Luc Montagnier. Identifying the virus was the first step toward developing tests and, eventually, the treatments that have turned HIV from a death sentence into a manageable chronic condition. It was a victory for virology in a time of great fear. 🔬
