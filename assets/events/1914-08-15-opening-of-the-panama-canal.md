The Panama Canal is often called the 'eighth wonder of the world.' It required cutting through a mountain range and building a massive system of locks to lift ships 85 feet over the Continental Divide. 

It was a brutal project that cost thousands of lives (mostly due to yellow fever and malaria), but it saved ships from the dangerous 8,000-mile journey around Cape Horn. It turned the Atlantic and Pacific into neighbors. 🚢
