After centuries of dreaming and several failed starts, a tunnel was finally bored 250 feet below the English Channel. 

In 1990, workers from both sides met in the middle and shook hands. When it opened in 1994, it allowed trains to go from London to Paris in just three hours. It’s the longest undersea tunnel in the world, proving that even a 20-mile stretch of water is no match for a really big drill and a lot of determination. 🚄
