In 1968, Douglas Engelbart sat on a stage in San Francisco and showed off a world that didn't exist yet: a computer with a mouse, windows, hypertext, and even video conferencing. 

At the time, computers were operated with punch cards and text. Engelbart showed that they could be interactive and intuitive. The mouse was just a little wooden box with two wheels, but it became the gateway to the modern user interface. It was a vision of the future that we're still living in today. 🖱️
