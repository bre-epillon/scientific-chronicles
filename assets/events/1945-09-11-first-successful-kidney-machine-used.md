During the Nazi occupation of the Netherlands, Willem Kolff was determined to save patients from kidney failure. With limited resources, he built the first dialysis machine using sausage skins, a cooling system from a Ford, and some slats from a Venetian blind. 

On September 11, 1945, it finally worked on a patient who was in a coma, saving her life. Kolff’s scrappy invention proved that organs could be supplemented by machines, sparking the entire field of artificial organs. It’s the ultimate example of 'making it work' under pressure. 🏥
