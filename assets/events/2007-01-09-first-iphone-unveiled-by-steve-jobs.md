At Macworld 2007, Steve Jobs told the crowd he was introducing a widescreen iPod with touch controls, a revolutionary mobile phone, and a breakthrough internet communications device. Then he revealed they were all the same thing: the iPhone. 

It wasn't the first smartphone, but it was the one that made the internet feel like it belonged in our pockets. It killed the physical keyboard and made 'multi-touch' a part of our daily vocabulary. Life after this day became much more 'app-centric.' 📲
