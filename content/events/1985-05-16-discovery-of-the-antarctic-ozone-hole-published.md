In the 1980s, Joe Farman and his team noticed that the ozone layer over Antarctica was thinning at an alarming rate. They realized that man-made chemicals called CFCs were eating away the gas that protects us from UV radiation. 

The discovery led to the Montreal Protocol, a rare moment of global cooperation that banned the offending chemicals. Today, the ozone hole is slowly healing. It’s a reminder that we can break the planet, but if we're smart and work together, we can also fix it. 🌍
