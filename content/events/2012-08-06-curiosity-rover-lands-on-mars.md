Landing a one-ton rover on Mars is hard. To do it, NASA used a 'sky crane' that lowered Curiosity on cables while hovering over the surface with rockets. It was nicknamed 'Seven Minutes of Terror.' 

Curiosity was sent to find out if Mars ever had the conditions for life. It found evidence of ancient stream beds and organic molecules. It's basically a mobile chemist that's been exploring Gale Crater for over a decade, occasionally singing 'Happy Birthday' to itself. 🤖
