In 1883, Charles Fritts built the first solar array on a New York City rooftop. He used selenium wafers coated with gold. 

The efficiency was terrible—only about 1%—but it proved that we could turn light directly into electricity with no moving parts. Fritts predicted that solar would eventually compete with coal. It took 140 years, but he's finally being proven right. He was the first person to realize that the sun is the ultimate power plant. ☀️
