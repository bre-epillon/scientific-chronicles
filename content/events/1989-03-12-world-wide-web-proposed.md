While working at CERN, Tim Berners-Lee wrote a memo titled 'Information Management: A Proposal.' His boss famously wrote 'Vague but exciting...' on the cover. 

That 'vague' idea became the World Wide Web—the system of HTML, HTTP, and URLs that we use to navigate the internet today. Tim didn't patent it; he gave it to the world for free, ensuring that everyone could have access to the sum of human knowledge (and millions of cat videos). 🌐
